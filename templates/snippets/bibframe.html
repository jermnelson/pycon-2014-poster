Linked Data BIBFRAME Vocabulary

BIBFRAME - short for Bibliographic Framework - is the work of the Library of Congress's Bibliographic Framework Initiative. BIBFRAME is intended to replace MARC21 in the next few years. This initiative started with a press release by the Library of Congress in November 2011. Since that time, a formal LinkedData vocabulary was released in January 2013 at http://bibframe.org followed most recently with a major revision of the vocabulary in the Fall of 2013.

The BIBFRAME model is built using RDF classes and properties and has four core classes: (taken from http://bibframe.org/vocab-model/)

  • Creative Work - a resource reflecting a conceptual essence of the cataloging resource.
  • Instance - resource reflecting an individual, material embodiment of the Work.
  • Authority - a resource reflecting key authority concepts that have defined relationships reflected in the Work and Instance. Example of Authority Resources include People, Places, Topics, Organizations
  • Annotation - a resource that enhances our knowledge about another resource when knowing, minimally, 'who' is doing the annotating is important. Library Holdings, Cover Art and Reviews are examples types.
